Data Visualization
In PowerBI

This Power BI project, covering the 2020-2021 academic year, analyzes the relationship between socio-economic factors and educational outcomes across school districts in Massachusetts, USA. The report focuses on three key areas:

  • Low-Income % vs. Average SAT Scores: Initially identified as a weak negative correlation, further analysis revealed a high correlation between the percentage of low-income students in a school district and lower average SAT scores, indicating that higher low-income populations are strongly associated with reduced SAT performance.
  • Median Household Income by School District: A table ranks the top 10 school districts by median household income, with Weston, MA, leading at $201,200, and Norfolk, MA, at $143,682. This highlights the economic disparity across districts.
  • Dropout Rates: The report identifies a total dropout count of 12,000 students across districts. Grade 11 leads with the highest dropout rate at 29.65% (646.1 students). The top 5 districts with the highest dropout rates are State Totals, Boston, Springfield, Brockton, and Lynn.
